The Question You Asked

When does this become profitable?

The honest answer
Under current behaviour — it doesn't. On the best realistic execution path, somewhere around July to November 2027. Roughly 15 to 19 months from today. And only if specific things change now.
I. First, A Reality Check

The early cohorts looked good. That wasn't wrong — it just wasn't enough.

November acquired players at €11 CPA and is on track to recover 92% of that spend within 12 months. December looked similar. If the world had stayed that way, this would be a different conversation. But in January the CPA began climbing, and by March it had nearly doubled. That is what broke the model.
Worth saying plainly

The intuition that this was close to working was reasonable. November's cohort is, in fact, close to breaking even — it just needs three more months. The problem is that each new month we've been adding more expensive cohorts on top that need far more time and far more revenue to recover. So the portfolio as a whole keeps drifting further from profitability, even as individual cohorts mature.

IV. What The Realistic Path Requires

Path C is the path. Here is everything that must change for it to work.

Path C isn't a guess — it is what the numbers require. CPA must fall, LTV must rise, and volume will likely come down as bad sources are cut. Each of these three moves is necessary. None is sufficient on its own.
01

CPA falls to €10

from €15.58 · −36%

Achievable by cutting the 1–2 traffic sources that have been driving the recent cost inflation. Likely the sources that also deliver the lowest-quality players — which is why cutting them helps CPA and LTV simultaneously.

02

Blended LTV rises to €14

from €10.26 · +36%

Happens automatically when bad sources are removed: the remaining volume is concentrated in better-performing sources. Could also be supported by negotiating a higher revshare percentage with Betway using the source-level LTV data as leverage.

03

Volume drops to 600 FTDs/mo

from ~900 · −33%

This is the uncomfortable part. Cutting expensive sources will reduce total volume. This is fine — it is better to acquire 600 FTDs profitably than 900 at a loss. Volume can be grown back later from a profitable base.

V. Two Ways To Read This

The honest version, and the better news nested inside it.

The Honest Version
Not in 2026. Probably not in the first half of 2027 either.
Even under the realistic best case, the portfolio stays in the red through all of 2026 and most of 2027. Monthly cash flow doesn't turn positive on the whole portfolio until roughly Q3 2027. The €49k already-lost amount is not recoverable quickly under any realistic scenario — it gets paid back over roughly 15–24 months of disciplined execution.

And if nothing changes, it is genuinely "never." Not a rhetorical never — an arithmetic one. At a €5.32 per-FTD gap, more volume is more loss.
The Nested Good News
Individual new cohorts can be profitable much sooner than the portfolio.
The €49k legacy deficit is sunk. New cohorts acquired under Path C economics (€10 CPA, €14 LTV) would be profitable within their own 6–8 month lifecycle. That means within Q4 2026 we could be looking at each monthly cohort being individually profitable — even while the overall portfolio is still paying off legacy.

That's the real operational milestone to target: positive contribution margin on new acquisitions before end of 2026. That's what proves the model works; the portfolio zero-line is just when the math catches up.
VI. The Cost of Waiting

Every month of delay costs roughly €9,000.

The current trajectory is not neutral — it actively destroys value month by month. Here is the difference between acting now vs. acting six months from now, assuming the same eventual execution.

Act now (May 2026)

Source pruning executed in May. CPA lands at €10, LTV rises to €14 over Q3. New cohorts from June onward are structurally profitable.

Portfolio break-even: around November 2027.
Total additional loss before break-even: approximately €49k (matching today's legacy).

Act in October 2026

Six more months of status-quo acquisition at €20 CPA. During that window, roughly €53k of additional loss accumulates. Source pruning still works once executed — but now it has to pay down €102k of legacy rather than €49k.

Portfolio break-even: pushed to approximately May 2028.
Net cost of six-month delay: ~€53k, and six additional months to recoup.

VII. To Close

This was a harder answer than you were hoping for. That doesn't make it the wrong answer.

What I'd tell your boss, in one paragraph

The November and December cohorts were performing well and a profitable account was within reach. Between January and March, CPA rose from €11 to over €20 while revenue per player stayed flat. That turned every new acquisition into a loss-maker. Continuing on autopilot, the portfolio never reaches profit. But if we act now — specifically, cutting the 1–2 traffic sources that caused the CPA inflation and likely also brought the weakest players — we can put new cohorts into individual profit by Q4 2026 and reach portfolio break-even around mid-to-late 2027. Delay costs roughly €9k per month.

The diagnosis is sharp. The fix is well-defined. The execution window is open. But the €49k already spent is gone, and no amount of patience with current-behaviour economics brings it back.
